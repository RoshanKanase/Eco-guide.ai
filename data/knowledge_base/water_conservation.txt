WATER CONSERVATION GUIDELINES FOR CAMPUSES AND HOSTELS

DAILY USAGE BENCHMARKS (INDIA):
- Shower: 10-15 litres per minute (limit to 5 minutes = 50-75L)
- Bucket bath: 15-20 litres (preferred over shower)
- Flushing toilet: 6-12 litres per flush (use half-flush when possible)
- Brushing teeth with tap running: 12 litres/minute (use mug = 0.5L)
- Washing clothes: 50-80 litres per load (full load only)
- Campus cafeteria per meal: 5-10 litres for cooking + cleaning

CAMPUS WATER SAVING TIPS:
1. Report leaking taps and pipes immediately - one drip per second wastes 10,000 litres/year
2. Install aerators on taps (reduces flow by 50% without affecting pressure)
3. Use rainwater harvesting where available
4. Reuse AC condensate water for gardening
5. Water plants in early morning or evening to reduce evaporation
6. Use broom instead of hose to clean outdoor areas

HOSTEL SPECIFIC:
- Take bucket baths instead of long showers
- Turn off tap while soaping hands or brushing teeth
- Collect RO reject water for mopping and toilet flushing
- Wash clothes only with full loads
- Do not waste drinking water on plants - use greywater

MONSOON & DROUGHT AWARENESS:
- During drought: limit showers to 3 minutes, no car/bike washing
- During monsoon: maintain harvesting systems, check for contamination
- Groundwater depletion affects 60% of Indian districts

AI DECISION SUPPORT RULES:
- If daily usage > 150L per person: HIGH consumption - recommend audit
- If usage 100-150L: MODERATE - suggest 3 specific improvements
- If usage < 100L: GOOD - maintain habits and help peers

MEASURABLE IMPACT:
- Fixing one leaking tap saves 10,000 litres annually
- Bucket bath vs 10-min shower saves 80 litres per bath
- A campus of 5000 students saving 20L/day each = 36.5 million litres/year
