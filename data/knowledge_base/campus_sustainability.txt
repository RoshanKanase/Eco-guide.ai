CAMPUS SUSTAINABILITY POLICIES AND GREEN CAMPUS INITIATIVES

GREEN CAMPUS FRAMEWORK (AICTE/UGC ALIGNED):
1. Energy efficiency in buildings
2. Water conservation and rainwater harvesting
3. Waste management with segregation at source
4. Green cover and biodiversity
5. Sustainable transport promotion
6. Environmental education and awareness

STUDENT ROLE IN SUSTAINABILITY:
- Participate in eco-clubs and green committees
- Volunteer for waste audit drives
- Advocate for plastic-free events
- Document and report sustainability issues via grievance systems
- Share knowledge with peers using digital tools

EVENT SUSTAINABILITY CHECKLIST:
- Use digital invitations, not printed flyers
- Provide water dispensers instead of bottled water
- Use steel/ceramic plates or biodegradable alternatives
- Segregate event waste at source
- Calculate and offset event carbon footprint

RESPONSIBLE AI IN SUSTAINABILITY PROJECTS:
- Use aggregated/anonymous data only for campus analytics
- Clearly label AI-generated advice as guidance, not regulation
- Avoid bias against communities with different resource access
- Provide transparent reasoning for recommendations
- Include human review for policy-level decisions

GRIEVANCE CATEGORIES FOR SUSTAINABLE CAMPUSES:
- Water wastage / leaking infrastructure
- Improper waste disposal
- Excessive energy use in labs/hostels
- Loss of green cover
- Air/noise pollution from construction
- Lack of recycling facilities

EXPECTED OUTCOMES OF AI-POWERED SUSTAINABILITY TOOLS:
- 30% improvement in waste segregation accuracy
- 20% reduction in per-capita water use through awareness
- Measurable carbon footprint tracking per department
- Faster access to policy information via RAG systems
- Increased student engagement in SDG-aligned activities
