WASTE SEGREGATION GUIDELINES - INDIAN CAMPUS & MUNICIPAL STANDARDS

DRY WASTE (BLUE BIN):
- Paper, cardboard, newspapers, notebooks
- Plastic bottles, containers, wrappers (clean and dry)
- Metal cans, aluminium foil (clean)
- Glass bottles and jars
- Tetra packs (rinsed and flattened)
- E-waste should NOT go in dry waste - use designated e-waste collection

WET WASTE (GREEN BIN):
- Food scraps, vegetable peels, fruit waste
- Tea leaves, coffee grounds
- Leftover cooked food
- Garden waste, leaves, flowers
- Biodegradable plates and cups (uncoated)

HAZARDOUS WASTE (RED BIN / SPECIAL COLLECTION):
- Batteries (all types)
- CFL bulbs, tube lights
- Paint cans, solvents
- Medical waste (syringes, medicines)
- Chemical containers
- E-waste: phones, chargers, laptops (separate e-waste drive)

REJECT WASTE (BLACK BIN):
- Sanitary napkins, diapers
- Soiled tissue paper
- Broken ceramics, thermocol
- Mixed/contaminated waste that cannot be recycled
- Dust from sweeping

CAMPUS BEST PRACTICES:
1. Rinse plastic and glass before disposal
2. Flatten cardboard boxes to save space
3. Separate caps from bottles when possible
4. Never mix wet and dry waste
5. Use cloth bags instead of single-use plastic
6. Compost wet waste on campus if facility exists

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Pizza boxes with grease → reject waste, not dry
- Chip packets with foil lining → reject or dry (check local rules)
- Styrofoam/thermocol → reject waste
- Wet paper → wet waste, not dry
- Biodegradable plastic needs industrial composting - do not mix with home compost

IMPACT:
Proper segregation increases recycling rate from 20% to 80%+
Reduces landfill burden and methane emissions from mixed organic waste
Enables circular economy on campus
